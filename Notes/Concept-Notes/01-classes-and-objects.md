## 📖 Overview

**Classes** and **Objects** are the foundation of Object-Oriented Programming (OOP) in Python.

A **class** acts as a blueprint that defines the attributes and behaviors of an object, while an **object** is an actual instance created from that blueprint.

Understanding this concept is essential before learning advanced OOP topics such as constructors, inheritance, polymorphism, encapsulation, and abstraction.
